ON ADVERSITY - Stoic Teachings

"The impediment to action advances action. What stands in the way becomes the way." -- Marcus Aurelius

"No man is more unhappy than he who never faces adversity. For he is not permitted to prove himself." -- Seneca

"Difficulties are things that show a person what they are." -- Epictetus

"You have passed through life without an opponent -- no one can ever know what you are capable of, not even you." -- Seneca

"The art of living is more like wrestling than dancing." -- Marcus Aurelius

"Fire tests gold, suffering tests brave men." -- Seneca

"It is during our darkest moments that we must focus to see the light." -- Aristotle

"He who fears death will never do anything worth of a man who is alive." -- Seneca

"Choose not to be harmed and you won't feel harmed. Don't feel harmed and you haven't been." -- Marcus Aurelius

"What really frightens and dismays us is not external events themselves, but the way in which we think about them." -- Epictetus

"The greater the difficulty, the more glory in surmounting it." -- Epicurus
