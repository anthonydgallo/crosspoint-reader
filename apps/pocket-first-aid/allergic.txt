ALLERGIC REACTIONS

MILD REACTION:
Signs: Hives, itching, minor swelling, runny nose, watery eyes

Treatment:
1. Remove the allergen if possible
2. Take antihistamine (Benadryl/diphenhydramine)
3. Apply cool compress to itchy areas
4. Monitor for worsening symptoms

ANAPHYLAXIS (SEVERE - EMERGENCY):
Signs: Difficulty breathing, throat swelling, wheezing, rapid pulse, dizziness, nausea/vomiting, widespread hives, drop in blood pressure, loss of consciousness

ANAPHYLAXIS TREATMENT:
1. CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY
2. Use EPINEPHRINE auto-injector (EpiPen) if available
   - Remove safety cap
   - Press firmly against outer thigh (through clothing is OK)
   - Hold for 10 seconds
   - Note the time
3. Have person lie down, elevate legs
4. If breathing is difficult, let them sit up
5. Give second EpiPen after 5-15 min if no improvement
6. Begin CPR if person stops breathing
7. Do NOT give oral medications if having trouble swallowing

COMMON TRIGGERS:
- Foods: peanuts, tree nuts, shellfish, milk, eggs
- Insect stings: bees, wasps, hornets
- Medications: penicillin, aspirin, NSAIDs
- Latex

PREVENTION:
- Carry EpiPen if prescribed
- Wear medical alert bracelet
- Read food labels carefully
