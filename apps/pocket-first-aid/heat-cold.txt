HEAT & COLD EMERGENCIES

--- HEAT EXHAUSTION ---
Signs: Heavy sweating, weakness, cool/clammy skin, nausea, dizziness, fast weak pulse

Treatment:
1. Move to cool place
2. Lie down, elevate legs
3. Apply cool wet cloths
4. Sip water slowly
5. If vomiting, seek medical help

--- HEAT STROKE (EMERGENCY) ---
Signs: High body temp (103F+), hot/red/dry skin, rapid strong pulse, confusion, unconsciousness

Treatment:
1. CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY
2. Move to cool area
3. Cool rapidly: cold water, ice packs at neck, armpits, groin
4. Do NOT give fluids if unconscious
5. Monitor breathing

--- HYPOTHERMIA ---
Signs: Shivering, confusion, slurred speech, drowsiness, slow breathing

Treatment:
1. Call 911 for severe cases
2. Move to warm area
3. Remove wet clothing
4. Warm center of body first (chest, neck, head, groin)
5. Use blankets, skin-to-skin contact
6. Give warm (not hot) drinks if conscious
7. Do NOT rub extremities

--- FROSTBITE ---
Signs: Numbness, white/grayish skin, hard/waxy feeling

Treatment:
1. Get to warm area
2. Do NOT rub the area
3. Soak in warm (not hot) water, 100-104F
4. Do NOT rewarm if refreezing is possible
5. Seek medical attention
