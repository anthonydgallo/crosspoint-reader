CPR - CARDIOPULMONARY RESUSCITATION

CALL 911 FIRST. Begin CPR immediately.

ADULT CPR (age 12+):

1. CHECK the scene is safe
2. TAP shoulders, shout "Are you OK?"
3. CALL 911 (or have someone call)
4. Place heel of one hand on center of chest, between the nipples
5. Place other hand on top, interlace fingers
6. Push HARD and FAST
   - Depth: at least 2 inches
   - Rate: 100-120 pushes per minute
   - Allow full chest recoil
7. After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths
   - Tilt head back, lift chin
   - Pinch nose, seal lips over mouth
   - Blow for 1 second each
8. Continue 30:2 cycle until help arrives

HANDS-ONLY CPR:
If untrained or uncomfortable with breaths, do chest compressions only at 100-120/min without stopping.

CHILD CPR (age 1-12):
Same as adult but use one hand for compressions. Push about 2 inches deep.

INFANT CPR (under 1 year):
Use 2 fingers on breastbone just below nipple line. Push about 1.5 inches deep. Cover mouth AND nose for breaths.

KEY: Push hard, push fast, don't stop.
