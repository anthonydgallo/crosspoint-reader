CHOKING RESPONSE

SIGNS: Cannot speak, cough, or breathe. May clutch throat. Skin may turn blue.

ADULT / CHILD (over 1 year):

1. ASK "Are you choking? Can I help?"
2. Stand behind the person
3. Make a fist with one hand
4. Place fist just above the navel, below the ribcage
5. Grasp fist with other hand
6. Give quick UPWARD thrusts
7. Repeat until object comes out or person becomes unconscious

IF PERSON BECOMES UNCONSCIOUS:
- Lower them to the ground
- Call 911
- Begin CPR
- Before giving breaths, look in mouth for the object
- If you see it, sweep it out

INFANT (under 1 year):
1. Place infant face-down on your forearm, supporting head
2. Give 5 firm back blows between shoulder blades with heel of hand
3. Turn infant face-up
4. Give 5 chest thrusts with 2 fingers on breastbone
5. Repeat back blows and chest thrusts
6. If infant becomes unconscious, begin infant CPR

CHOKING ALONE (self):
- Make a fist above your navel
- Thrust upward
- Or lean over a chair back and press firmly

PREVENTION: Cut food small for children. Supervise meals. Chew thoroughly.
