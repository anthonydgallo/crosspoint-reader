BURNS TREATMENT

FIRST DEGREE (red, painful, no blisters):
- Run COOL water over burn for 10-20 min
- Do NOT use ice
- Apply aloe vera or moisturizer
- Take pain reliever if needed
- Cover loosely with sterile gauze

SECOND DEGREE (blisters, very painful):
- Run COOL water over burn for 10-20 min
- Do NOT pop blisters
- Apply antibiotic ointment
- Cover with non-stick sterile bandage
- Seek medical attention if larger than 3 inches or on face, hands, feet, or joints

THIRD DEGREE (white/charred, may be painless):
- CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY
- Do NOT remove clothing stuck to burn
- Do NOT apply water, ointment, or ice
- Cover loosely with sterile bandage
- Monitor for shock
- Keep person warm

CHEMICAL BURNS:
- Remove contaminated clothing
- Flush with large amounts of water for 20+ min
- Call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222

ELECTRICAL BURNS:
- Do NOT touch person if still in contact with source
- Call 911
- Check breathing and pulse
- Treat visible burns

NEVER: Use butter, toothpaste, or oil on burns.
ALWAYS: Seek medical help for serious burns.
