BITES & STINGS

--- SNAKE BITE ---
1. Call 911
2. Keep person calm and still
3. Remove jewelry near the bite
4. Keep bite below heart level
5. Do NOT cut the wound
6. Do NOT try to suck out venom
7. Do NOT apply ice or tourniquet
8. Note the snake's appearance if safe

--- SPIDER BITE ---
1. Clean with soap and water
2. Apply ice wrapped in cloth
3. Elevate the area
4. Seek medical help for: severe pain, cramps, spreading redness, or if bitten by black widow or brown recluse

--- BEE / WASP STING ---
1. Remove stinger by scraping with flat edge (credit card)
2. Do NOT squeeze stinger
3. Wash with soap and water
4. Apply ice for 10 minutes
5. Take antihistamine for swelling
6. Watch for allergic reaction (see Allergic Reactions)

--- TICK BITE ---
1. Grasp tick close to skin with fine tweezers
2. Pull straight up with steady pressure
3. Do NOT twist, crush, or burn the tick
4. Clean area with rubbing alcohol
5. Save the tick in a sealed bag
6. Watch for rash or fever for 30 days
7. Seek help if bull's-eye rash appears

--- ANIMAL BITE ---
1. Control bleeding with pressure
2. Wash thoroughly with soap and water for 5 min
3. Apply antibiotic ointment
4. Seek medical attention (may need rabies evaluation)
5. Report the bite to local animal control
